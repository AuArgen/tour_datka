About This Tour

Experience Bishkek's Living History

Perfect for newcomers, this half-day tour is a comfortable and insightful way to see Bishkek's most famous spots — from Ala-Too Square to Osh Bazaar. With a private air-conditioned car and an English-speaking guide, you'll receive personal stories behind each sight.

Learn about Kyrgyzstan's rich history, admire the heroic statues, and view the magnificent Soviet-era architecture. This tour also includes a delicious lunch with local dishes at a national restaurant, completing your experience of Kyrgyz culture.

Set against the backdrop of Central Asia's Tian Shan mountains, Bishkek abounds in spacious squares, wide boulevards, marble-faced public buildings and monuments that reflect the country's fascinating history. With over 30 attractions on the route, every corner tells a story.

Step by Step

What to Expect

1
Osh Bazaar
Our guide picks you up from your hotel. First stop is the legendary open-air Osh Bazaar — a vibrant market where you can find everything from food and spices to souvenirs and musical instruments. Immerse yourself in the sights, smells and energy of authentic Kyrgyz life.
2
Kyrgyz National Philharmonic Hall
Marvel at the charming architecture of the Philharmonic — a white marble building on a hill that recalls an antique temple, adorned with colourful stained glass and hammered copper details.
3
The White House
The seat of Kyrgyz government. Your guide shares the dramatic stories of the 2005 Tulip Revolution and the 2010 riots — historic moments etched into the national memory.
4
Ala-Too Square
The main square of Bishkek — home to the State Historical Museum (90,000 exhibits), Oak Park, a 45-metre flagpole, and the iconic 10-metre equestrian statue of the legendary hero Manas.
5
Opera and Ballet Theatre
A Soviet-era landmark at the heart of the city. Admire the classical facade and the rich interior paintings by Kyrgyz artists — a testament to the country's deep artistic heritage.
6
Victory Square
Three massive granite arches forming the silhouette of a yurt. At their centre stands a mother figure with a cup, guarding the eternal flame — a memorial to those who fell in World War II.
7
Central Mosque & Lunch
Visit one of Central Asia's largest mosques — built in Ottoman revival style with four minarets. Then enjoy a delicious traditional Kyrgyz lunch at a national restaurant before being dropped off at your hotel.
