About This Tour

Into the Tian Shan Wilderness

Ala Archa National Park is one of Central Asia's most spectacular natural reserves — just 40 km from Bishkek, yet an entirely different world. Alpine meadows, roaring rivers, glaciers, and peaks rising above 4,800 metres create a landscape that takes your breath away.

There are numerous hiking options in the park, from gentle strolls along the Ala-Archa valley floor to serious mountaineering approaches. You choose the level; we provide the perfect guide. In addition to mountains and rivers, discover the park's unique and rich fauna and flora.

Snow leopards, wolves, and golden eagles inhabit this park. While spotting the big cats is rare, the birdsong, wildflowers and mountain views are an experience unto themselves.

Step by Step

What to Expect

1
Departure from Bishkek
Pick-up from your hotel at 9:00 AM. The drive to Ala Archa takes around 45 minutes through the suburbs and foothills — a scenic introduction to the Kyrgyz mountains.
2
Park Entrance & Trail Briefing
Enter the national park and meet your nature guide. Together you'll choose the trail that matches your fitness level — from the valley floor at 1,600 m to higher alpine routes at 2,800 m+.
3
Ala-Archa River Valley
Follow the crystal-clear glacial river upstream. Your guide points out local flora — juniper, spruce forest, wildflowers — and explains the ecology of this protected reserve.
4
Ak-Say Waterfall & Alpine Zone
Reach the spectacular Ak-Say waterfall fed by glacial meltwater. For those continuing higher, the Ratsek hut at 3,300 m offers panoramic glacier views and is the gateway to peak climbing.
5
Picnic Lunch in the Mountains
Pause at a scenic viewpoint for a prepared picnic lunch. Eat surrounded by towering peaks, listening to birdsong and the rush of mountain water — one of the best lunches of your life.
6
Return & Hotel Drop-Off
Descend through the valley as the afternoon light turns the mountains golden. Return to Bishkek around 5–6 PM, tired in the best possible way.
